04Patterns & texture

Quiet grids. Loud edges.

Most surfaces stay clean. When a surface needs depth, it gets one of six patterns. Never two. Applied with restraint. Patterns reinforce structure, they never decorate.

Spectrum grid · primary brand surface

The six patterns

Four surfaces · one per moment · never combined
Grid · DarkSURFACE-01
Grid · LightSURFACE-02
ScanlinesSURFACE-03
Spectrum gridSURFACE-04
PRINCIPLE 01

Patterns reinforce structure.

Use a grid to imply order and a system underneath. Use scanlines to imply telemetry, signal, motion. Patterns should always have a reason, not as decoration.

PRINCIPLE 02

One pattern per surface.

Layering patterns dilutes them. A surface either has a grid, or it doesn't. The spectrum grid is the only exception. It's a brand surface, used sparingly for hero moments.

PRINCIPLE 03

Quiet by default.

Pattern lines are 6–8% white on dark, slate-200 on light. They're meant to be felt, not seen. If the pattern reads before the content, the opacity is wrong.

PRINCIPLE 04

Never inside product UI.

Patterns belong to brand surfaces: marketing pages, decks, hero blocks. The product UI itself stays flat: white, slate, and the occasional accent. No grids behind tables.

05Roundness

One radius. Four pixels.

EULER has a single corner radius across the entire system. Buttons, cards, inputs, modals, badges. All 4px. It's the fastest visual cue that something is ours.

The canonical radius

Squared, with a hairline of softness.

Sharp enough to feel structural. Just round enough to not feel hostile. 4px sits at the edge of "is that radius?", and that's exactly the point. It signals system, not whimsy.

06Tone of voice & personality

Calm but firm. Sharp, not flashy.

EULER speaks the way an experienced operator does. Straight to the point, quietly confident, skeptical of fluff. Controlled intensity. Clear, no filler. Educational, grounded in data, never theatrical.

MC
Brand voice persona

Mark Cuban

If EULER had a voice, it would be his. Operationally grounded, dry-edged, sharp without being abrasive. The voice of someone who's run the business, knows how it breaks, and has no time for buzzwords.

Cadence & rhythm

How a EULER sentence moves
01
Starts with the point quickly. Little to no runway before the tension appears.
02
Keeps momentum high through short-to-medium sentences, not long explanations.
03
Uses controlled urgency. It should feel pressing, but never frantic.
04
Carries a quiet edge. Slight skepticism and realism sharpen the tone.
05
Builds pressure through contrast. What's broken vs. what changes.
06
Sounds aware. Every claim should feel tied to time, revenue, scale, or efficiency.
07
Uses repetition strategically to build emphasis, not theatrics.
08
Feels grounded and operator-led. Never airy, abstract, or detached from execution.
09
Maintains emotional restraint. Confident and assertive without sounding loud.
10
Ends with clarity and forward motion. The reader should feel momentum, not ambiguity.
